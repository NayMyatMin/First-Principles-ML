Not commutative AB ≠ BA

AB ≠ BA in general (BA may not even be defined)

ML connection: Layer order matters. A network computing W₂(W₁x) is different from W₁(W₂x). Swapping layers changes the function entirely.

Associative A(BC) = (AB)C

A(BC) = (AB)C — grouping doesn't matter

ML connection: You can compute W₂(W₁x) as a sequence of mat-vec products (cheap) or first compute (W₂W₁) then multiply by x. Same result, different cost. Choose the cheaper path.

Distributive A(B+C) = AB + AC

A(B + C) = AB + AC

ML connection: Used in deriving gradient expressions. If the loss depends on two terms, you can distribute the upstream gradient across them independently.

Transpose reverses order (AB)ᵀ = BᵀAᵀ

(AB)ᵀ = BᵀAᵀ — the order flips!

ML connection: This shows up directly in backpropagation. When computing gradients, you need transposed weight matrices, and this identity tells you the order reverses through the chain rule. This is the most practically important property for deep learning.
